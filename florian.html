I come from the poorest family in a poor village in Kakumiro. No food, no money,no hope for going to school. Everyday Iwatched my mother struggle with pain her bones.She'd bend over a bucket; her face strained in agony as she washed. My father was mentally ill so he was unable to provide for our family.As theoldest child,I felt responsible for improving our lives and getting us out of of poverty. I would collect jackfruit from the fields to fill our bellies. We children huddled together in the same bed to keep warm. I dreamed of away to rise out of this terrible poverty,but there seemed no way

One day my family recieved exciting new from my cousin who moved to Kampala send "Angella here to city" Cousin argued." Angella can earn money and send it to you."
